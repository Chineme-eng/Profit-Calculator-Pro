What actually moves your margin
01
Cut your biggest cost first
Shaving 5% off your largest line item does more than eliminating ten small ones. Always attack the tallest bar in your chart.
02
Revenue ≠ profit
A $50k revenue day with 8% margin earns you $4k. A $10k revenue day with 40% margin earns you $4k. Same money, very different businesses.
03
Digital scales, physical doesn't
Digital products and SaaS can reach 60–80% margins because the cost to serve the 1,000th customer is nearly zero. Physical products hit a ceiling.
04
Ad spend is a variable cost
Every dollar in ad spend should return more than a dollar in margin. If your ROAS drops below your break-even ROAS, you're paying to lose money.
05
Tax is a cost too
Self-employment tax (15.3% in the US), income tax, and sales tax all come off profit. Model tax as a cost line from day one or you'll get a painful surprise.
06
Retainers beat one-off sales
Recurring revenue means lower customer acquisition cost per dollar earned. One client paying $1k/month is worth more than twelve clients paying $1k once.
